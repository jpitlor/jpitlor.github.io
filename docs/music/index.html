USS Indiana Commissioning Gala

University Choir

In 2018, I was incredible fortunate to have been given an opportunity to fly from Purdue to Cape Canaveral to sing at the gala and ceremony for the commissioning of the USS Indiana, the US Navy's newest (at the time) submarine.

This video is a compilation of all the songs we sang. The next morning, we sang the star spangled banner again at the ceremony.

The video quality is pretty low as it was filmed using a cell phone, but the audio quality is much better.

Composition

I occasionally compose and arrange music. I don't do it in any professional capacity, but music is meant to be listened to, so here are some of my favorite compositions and arrangements.

Almost 80s Medley

In PMO, we had a tradition where before every Christmas Show performance, an ensemble would perform a parody of a popular song with lyrics that referenced inside jokes to hype everyone up for the upcoming performance. They are aptly named Fireups.

My senior year, I arranged my choir's fireup as a medley of Every Breath You Take (The Police), Africa (Toto), and Mamma Mia (ABBA). The entire arrangement had to be learned in 45 minutes, but I think the choir did a great job considering the constraints.

Ice Cream Sundae

My senior year at Purdue, I took Music Composition as my elective for my music minor. Our final project was to compose a string quartet, and we were fortunate enough to be able to record our projects with help from a string quartet from Purdue Bands and Orchestra and sound engineers that volunteer at the theatre building's recording studio.

The piece was sightread, so the recording isn't perfect, but the quartet still did a great job with it.
